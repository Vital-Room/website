The Vital Room Ltd - Data Protection and Privacy

1. GDPR & Data Protection Policy (ThanksDoc)

The Vital Room Ltd operates under the GDPR and data protection framework of ThanksDoc, a CQC-registered provider. All services and data handling are overseen by ThanksDoc to ensure compliance with UK data protection laws. Below is a summary of that framework, followed by details of how we handle your data.

Purpose

To ensure compliance with GDPR and data protection laws, protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and security of patient and staff data.

Key Principles

  • Lawful, Fair, and Transparent Processing: Data must be processed lawfully, fairly, and transparently.
  • Data Minimisation: Data collected must be relevant, accurate, and kept up to date.
  • Data Security: Personal data must be securely stored and only shared when lawful.
  • Individual Rights: Individuals have rights over their data, including access and correction.
  • Breach Reporting: Data breaches must be reported and investigated promptly.

Reporting Procedure

  • Immediate Reporting: All suspected data breaches must be reported immediately to the Data Controller (Registered Manager, Dr Linda Odogwu).
  • Email Notification: Email details of incidents to dataprotection@thanksdoc.co.uk within 24 hours.
  • Acknowledgement and Investigation: Incidents will be acknowledged immediately and investigated.
  • ICO Notification: Serious breaches must be reported to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) within 72 hours.
  • Individual Notification: Affected individuals must be informed where required under law.
  • Record Keeping: Records must be kept of all breaches, investigations, and corrective actions.
  • Subject Access Requests: Must be handled within statutory timeframes (usually 1 month).

When to Escalate

  • Any suspected or actual data breach.
  • Loss or theft of patient or staff data.
  • Unauthorised disclosure of confidential information.
  • Serious breaches requiring ICO notification.

Responsibilities

  • Registered Manager/Data Controller: Ensure GDPR compliance, oversee data protection, investigate breaches.
  • All Staff: Handle personal data lawfully, keep records accurate, and report breaches immediately.
  • Organisation: Provide training, ensure security systems, and review compliance regularly.

Reference

This summary is provided for guidance only. The full CQC-compliant policy is maintained by ThanksDoc and reviewed annually.

2. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how The Vital Room Ltd ("we", "us", "our", or "the Company") collects, uses, and protects your personal information when you use our services or visit our website. We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your personal data in accordance with UK data protection laws.

3. Information We Collect

3.1 Personal Information

We may collect the following types of personal information:

  • Name, date of birth, and contact details (email, phone, address)
  • Medical history, health conditions, and symptoms
  • Lifestyle information (diet, exercise, sleep patterns)
  • Biomarker test results and health assessments
  • Payment and billing information
  • Communication preferences and correspondence

3.2 Website Usage Information

When you visit our website, we may collect:

  • IP address and browser information
  • Pages visited and time spent on our site
  • Referral source and search terms used
  • Device information and operating system

4. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal information for the following purposes:

  • Providing lifestyle medicine services and health consultations
  • Creating personalised health and wellness plans
  • Processing payments and managing your account
  • Communicating with you about appointments and services
  • Improving our services and website functionality
  • Complying with legal and regulatory requirements
  • Marketing (with your explicit consent)

5. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data under the following legal bases:

  • Consent: When you have given clear consent for specific processing activities
  • Contract: To perform our contractual obligations to you
  • Legal Obligation: To comply with legal requirements (e.g., medical record keeping)
  • Legitimate Interests: For our legitimate business interests, balanced against your rights

6. Data Sharing and International Transfers

We may share your personal information with third-party service providers who assist us in providing our services.

We may also share your information with:

  • Healthcare providers, with your consent, to coordinate your care
  • Legal authorities, when required by law or to protect rights and safety

Some service providers may store or process personal data outside the United Kingdom. In such cases, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place in accordance with UK data protection law.

We do not sell your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes.

7. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data:

  • Encryption of sensitive data in transit and at rest
  • Secure servers and databases with restricted access
  • Regular security assessments and updates
  • Staff training on data protection and confidentiality
  • Secure disposal of data when no longer needed

8. Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as necessary to:

  • Provide our services to you
  • Comply with legal and regulatory requirements
  • Resolve disputes and enforce our agreements

Medical records are typically retained for 8 years after the last consultation, as required by medical regulations.

9. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access: Request copies of your personal data
  • Rectification: Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Erasure: Request deletion of your data (subject to legal requirements)
  • Restriction: Limit how we process your data
  • Portability: Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Objection: Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent for consent-based processing

To exercise these rights, please contact us at hello@thevitalroom.com.

10. International Data Transfers

Where personal data is transferred outside the UK, we ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place, in accordance with UK data protection law.

11. Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed to children under 16. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 16 without parental consent. If we become aware of such collection, we will delete the information.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any significant changes by email or through our website. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

13. Complaints
